ABOUT

Traditional Tripuri cuisine, known as Mui Borok, is the main cuisine of Tripura. People of Tripura are mostly non-vegetarian, so their food is mainly prepared with meat with added vegetables. Tripuri foodâ€™s main ingredient is called Berma, a small, oil-pasted, and dry fermented fish. The foods are usually prepared without oil, and so are considered to be healthy. Along with the Tripuri traditional cuisine, the food of Tripura is also influenced by Chinese and Bangladeshi cuisine. The Chinese food served in Tripura is hot and spicy as compared to authentic Chinese food.

Top varities of food in this state are:

  • Mui Borok
  • Kosai Bwtwi
  • Wahan Mosdeng
  • Gudok
  • Mosdeng Serma

MUI BOROK

It is the traditional and national food of Tripura. The dish is prepared from the main ingredient of Tripura, which is Berma. This dish is cooked without oil, and so is considered extremely healthy. Berma in the food enhances the salty taste buds as it is both a bit salty and spicy in flavor.

KOSAI BWTWI

This Tripuri dish is also prepared using Berma, the fermented fish, chopped onions, green chili, tofu pieces, garlic cloves, salt, and turmeric powder. To enhance the flavor, Khundrupui leaves are also added to Kosai Bwtwi.

WAHAN MOSDENG

It is also one of the traditional dishes of Tripura, which is prepared with pork, onion, coriander leaves, and green chili. The dish is delicious and lip-smacking with the addition of Green chili and local spices. If you are a meat lover, then this dish is truly a delight for your taste buds.

GUDOK

A traditional dish prepared by boiling vegetables and fermented fish. This dish is mainly prepared during festivals and special occasions.

MOSDENG SERMA

A tomato chutney prepared with Berma, red chili, and garlic is the Mosdeng Serma. The chutney being spicy and tangy enhances the flavors of other dishes.
